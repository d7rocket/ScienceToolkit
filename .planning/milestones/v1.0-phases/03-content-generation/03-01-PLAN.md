Read the current SKILL.md. Replace ONLY the `## Step 5: Generate carousel output` section (everything from that heading until `## Step 6`). Keep Steps 1-4 and Steps 6-7 exactly as they are.

The new Step 5 must contain these numbered rules, written as precise instructions Claude will follow at runtime:

**Rule 1: Finding selection** (prioritizes what the carousel focuses on)
- Priority order: (1) Cross-validated finding (academic + news on same topic), (2) Most counterintuitive/surprising result, (3) Most quantitatively specific (has a memorable stat), (4) Most recent
- Build the entire carousel around this single focal finding. Do not try to cover all sources equally — other sources inform caption depth and citations.

**Rule 2: Slide generation** (CONT-01, CONT-02, CONT-03, CONT-06)
- Generate 5-7 slides using `## Slide N: [Descriptive Title]` headings
- Slide 1 (Hook): Under 10 words. Question or surprising fact. Emoji allowed. Example: "What if everything we knew was wrong? JWST found out."
- Slides 2 through N-1 (Body): ~150 characters each, 2-3 short punchy sentences. No emoji. Each MUST end with a cliff-hanger or question. Use patterns like: "But there's a catch.", "That's not the weird part.", "Scientists weren't expecting what came next." Each cliff-hanger must connect forward — the next slide must resolve or escalate it.
- Slide N (Final — Takeaway + CTA): One-sentence key takeaway + CTA line like "Follow for daily science drops." Emoji allowed. Do NOT put a cliff-hanger on the final slide.
- One statistic per slide maximum
- Voice: cool professor register — calm confidence, dry wit allowed, authoritative but approachable. "Here's what that actually means." energy. High school reading level. When technical terms are unavoidable, add an inline plain-English gloss (e.g., "redshift (the stretching of light as objects move away)"). Slides are punchier and bolder than the caption.

**Rule 3: Caption generation** (CONT-04, CONT-06)
- 3-5 paragraphs following this arc:
  - Paragraph 1 (Hook): Question that includes the topic keyword. Podcast-episode-title energy.
  - Paragraph 2 (Context): Background needed to appreciate the finding. 2-3 sentences.
  - Paragraph 3 (Finding): The discovery stated clearly. Name-drop journal/outlet naturally using the `journal_or_outlet` field from the source list — e.g., "A study published in *{source.journal_or_outlet}* found that..." Do NOT use journal names from memory; only from the fetched source list.
  - Paragraph 4 (Significance): Why it matters, what it changes, what it challenges.
  - Paragraph 5 (Close): Forward-looking question that invites engagement. No CTA in the caption (CTA is on the final slide only).
- Target 400-600 words
- Hard ceiling: 2,100 characters. After drafting, count the characters in the caption. If over 2,100, shorten sentences in the significance or context paragraphs. Check characters, not words — a 600-word caption can exceed 2,100 chars.
- Caption tone relaxes into a more explanatory, flowing style compared to slides, but stays in the cool-professor register.
- No inline citation references — text reads clean. All citations live in the Sources section only.

**Rule 4: Hashtag generation** (CONT-05)
- Generate EXACTLY 5 hashtags — no more, no less
- All on one line, space-separated, no commas, no trailing punctuation
- Selection strategy: 1 topic-specific, 1 field-level, 1 broad science, 1 platform reach, 1 trending/topical
- Example: `#JamesWebbTelescope #Astrophysics #SpaceScience #GalaxyFormation #ScienceExplained`

**Rule 5: Citation formatting** (CITE-01, CITE-02)
- Number each source. Use the citation variant matching its type:

  Variant A — Published academic paper (peer_review_label contains "Published in"):
  ```
  N. {authors} ({year}). {title}. *{journal_or_outlet}*.
     DOI: https://doi.org/{doi}
     URL: {url}
     {peer_review_label}
  ```
  Include DOI line ONLY when doi is not None. Omit volume/issue/pages rather than inventing them.

  Variant B — Preprint (peer_review_label is "[Preprint - not peer reviewed]"):
  ```
  N. {authors} ({year}). {title}. *arXiv*.
     URL: {url}
     [Preprint - not peer reviewed]
  ```

  Variant C — News article (peer_review_label is "[News article]"):
  ```
  N. {authors} ({year}). {title}. *{journal_or_outlet}*.
     URL: {url}
     [News article]
  ```

- Every citation MUST have a clickable URL line.
- Use data from the structured source list only — never fabricate authors, DOIs, or journal names.

**Rule 6: Image output** (CITE-03)
- Collect ALL non-None `image_url` values from the structured source list
- Output each on its own line with a leading dash in the `## Images` section
- Do not add license labels in the Images section (labels are already in the Sources section)
- If no images were found in any source, write: "No source images available from fetched sources."
- Prefer listing CC-licensed images first when ordering.

**Section ordering** (must match examples/output-sample.md):
```
# [Topic Title]
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD | **Field:** [Field] | **Sources:** N
---
## Slide 1: [Title]
...
## Slide N: [Title]
...
---
## Caption
...
---
## Hashtags
...
---
## Sources
...
---
## Images
...
```

Write the new Step 5 as clear, numbered instruction prose that Claude follows at runtime. Use the rule numbers (Rule 1 through Rule 6) as subsection headers within Step 5. Keep instructions imperative and unambiguous.
